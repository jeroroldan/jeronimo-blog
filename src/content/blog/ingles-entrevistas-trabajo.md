# Inglés para Entrevistas de Trabajo en Estados Unidos

Aprender inglés general es útil, pero conseguir trabajo en Estados Unidos requiere un tipo de inglés más específico: el que se usa para vender tu experiencia, explicar decisiones técnicas, manejar presión y demostrar que podés colaborar con un equipo.

Esta guía está pensada para entrevistas en inglés para puestos técnicos, remotos, híbridos o presenciales en empresas estadounidenses. No se trata de memorizar respuestas perfectas. Se trata de tener estructuras claras para responder con seguridad, incluso si no entendés cada palabra.

---

## Objetivo de esta guía

Al terminar esta guía vas a poder:

- Presentarte profesionalmente en inglés.
- Responder preguntas comunes de entrevistas.
- Usar el método STAR para contar experiencias.
- Explicar proyectos técnicos de forma clara.
- Hacer preguntas inteligentes al entrevistador.
- Manejar frases cuando no entendés algo.
- Evitar errores comunes en entrevistas en inglés.
- Practicar con ejercicios concretos antes de aplicar.

---

## La mentalidad correcta

Una entrevista laboral en Estados Unidos no es un examen de inglés. Es una conversación profesional.

Tu objetivo no es sonar como un hablante nativo. Tu objetivo es comunicar tres cosas:

1. Tenés las habilidades necesarias.
2. Podés resolver problemas.
3. Podés trabajar bien con otras personas.

Si tu inglés no es perfecto, pero sos claro, preparado y profesional, tenés chances reales.

---

## La estructura base de una buena respuesta

En una entrevista, una respuesta fuerte suele tener esta forma:

```
Respuesta clara:
1. Direct answer
2. Contexto breve
3. Acción concreta
4. Resultado medible
5. Aprendizaje o conexión con el puesto
```

Ejemplo:

> Yes, I have experience working with React. In my last project, I built a dashboard for internal users using React, TypeScript and REST APIs. My main responsibility was improving the performance of the main table, and we reduced the loading time by around 40%. I think that experience would be useful for this role because your team is working on data-heavy interfaces.

Esta estructura funciona porque es simple, profesional y fácil de seguir.

## Presentación profesional: Tell me about yourself

Una de las preguntas más comunes es:

> Tell me about yourself.

No cuentes tu vida personal. No hables de todo lo que hiciste desde la escuela. Usá una presentación corta de 60 a 90 segundos.

### Fórmula recomendada

```
Presentación profesional:
1. Quién soy
2. Qué experiencia tengo
3. Qué tecnologías o habilidades domino
4. Qué tipo de problemas me gusta resolver
5. Por qué me interesa esta oportunidad
```

### Plantilla

```
My name is [name], and I'm a [role] with [number] years of experience in [area].
I have worked mainly with [technologies/tools].
Most recently, I worked on [project/responsibility], where I [impact/result].
I enjoy solving problems related to [problem type], and I'm looking for an opportunity where I can contribute to [company/team goal].
That's why this role caught my attention.
```

### Ejemplo completo

> My name is Jerónimo, and I'm a software developer with experience building backend systems, APIs and web applications. I have worked with technologies like Node.js, TypeScript, React, PostgreSQL and Docker. Most recently, I worked on improving backend performance and designing cleaner APIs for internal tools. I enjoy solving problems that involve scalability, clean architecture and automation. That's why this role caught my attention, because it seems connected to building reliable software for real users.

## Respuestas cortas para empezar

Si estás nervioso, empezá con frases simples. No hace falta una apertura sofisticada.

```
Sure, I'd be happy to.
Absolutely.
Of course.
Yes, definitely.
That's a good question.
I can explain that.
Let me give you a quick example.
```

Estas frases te dan medio segundo para ordenar la idea.

## Frases para ganar tiempo

En entrevistas en inglés, no pasa nada si necesitás pensar. Lo importante es no quedarte en silencio total.

```
Let me think about that for a second.
That's a good question. Let me organize my thoughts.
I want to give you a clear example.
Let me think of the best example from my experience.
I haven't encountered that exact situation, but I can explain how I would approach it.
```

Evitá decir:

```
I don't know.
I forgot.
I'm nervous.
My English is bad.
```

Si no sabés algo, decilo con profesionalismo:

```
I don't have direct experience with that specific tool, but I have worked with similar concepts and I'm confident I can learn it quickly.
```

## Preguntas más comunes y cómo responderlas

### 1. Why do you want to work here?

El entrevistador quiere saber si entendés la empresa y si tu motivación es real.

#### Mala respuesta

> Because I need a job.

Aunque sea verdad, no es una respuesta de entrevista.

#### Respuesta mejor

```
I'm interested in this role because I like the way your company is solving [specific problem].
I also saw that your team works with [technology/domain], which is aligned with my experience.
I believe I can contribute with my background in [skill] and learn from the team at the same time.
```

#### Ejemplo

> I'm interested in this role because I like the way your company is solving problems in the healthcare space. I also saw that your team works with React and Node.js, which is aligned with my experience. I believe I can contribute with my background in API development and clean frontend architecture while learning from a more experienced team.

---

### 2. What are your strengths?

No respondas con una palabra suelta. Conectá la fortaleza con el trabajo.

#### Fórmula

```
Strength + example + value for the role
```

#### Ejemplo

> One of my strengths is problem-solving. When I face a bug or a complex requirement, I try to break it down into smaller parts, reproduce the issue, check the logs and isolate the cause. This helps me solve problems faster and communicate the situation clearly to the team.

---

### 3. What are your weaknesses?

No digas algo que destruya tu candidatura, como:

```
I'm lazy.
I get angry easily.
I don't like teamwork.
I hate deadlines.
```

Tampoco uses una respuesta falsa como:

```
I work too hard.
I'm a perfectionist.
```

#### Fórmula segura

```
Weakness + what you're doing to improve it
```

#### Ejemplo

> One area I've been working on is speaking up earlier when I see risks in a project. In the past, I used to spend too much time trying to solve things alone. Now I try to communicate blockers earlier and ask for feedback before the issue becomes bigger.

---

### 4. Tell me about a challenging project

Esta pregunta es ideal para usar el método STAR.

#### Método STAR

```
S - Situation: contexto del problema
T - Task: tu responsabilidad
A - Action: qué hiciste
R - Result: resultado medible o aprendizaje
```

#### Plantilla

```
In my previous role, we had a situation where [context].
My responsibility was to [task].
To solve it, I [action 1], [action 2], and [action 3].
As a result, [result].
I learned that [lesson].
```

#### Ejemplo

> In my previous role, we had a situation where the API was taking too long to respond during peak hours. My responsibility was to identify the bottleneck and improve performance. I checked the logs, analyzed the slow queries, added indexes to the database and optimized one endpoint by reducing unnecessary requests. As a result, we reduced the response time by around 35%. I learned that performance improvements usually start with good measurement, not guesses.

---

### 5. Why should we hire you?

Esta pregunta mide confianza y claridad.

#### Fórmula

```
Experiencia + habilidades + actitud + encaje con el rol
```

#### Ejemplo

> I think you should hire me because I bring a solid combination of technical skills, problem-solving ability and willingness to learn. I have experience building APIs, working with databases and collaborating with frontend developers. I'm also comfortable asking questions, documenting my work and communicating progress. I believe I can start contributing quickly while continuing to grow with the team.

---

### 6. Where do you see yourself in five years?

No hace falta una respuesta perfecta. Mostrá dirección.

#### Ejemplo

> In five years, I see myself growing as a senior developer, taking more ownership of technical decisions and mentoring other developers. I'd like to become stronger in system design, architecture and communication. Ideally, I want to be in a role where I can solve complex problems and help the team deliver better software.

---

### 7. Why are you leaving your current job?

Evitá hablar mal de tu empresa, jefe o compañeros.

#### Fórmula

```
Agradecimiento + búsqueda de crecimiento + conexión con nueva oportunidad
```

#### Ejemplo

> I've learned a lot in my current role, and I'm grateful for the experience. At the same time, I'm looking for a new challenge where I can work with more complex systems, grow in English-speaking environments and contribute to a larger engineering team. This role seems like a good fit for that next step.

---

### 8. What is your expected salary?

Esta pregunta puede ser incómoda. No des un número demasiado rápido si no tenés información.

#### Respuesta flexible

> I'm open to discussing compensation based on the full package, including responsibilities, benefits, remote flexibility and growth opportunities. Do you have a budget range for this role?

#### Si necesitás dar rango

> Based on my research and experience, I'm looking for a range between [X] and [Y], depending on the full compensation package.

## Preguntas técnicas en inglés

Las entrevistas técnicas suelen tener preguntas como:

```
Can you explain how this works?
What happens when this endpoint is called?
How would you debug this issue?
How would you design this feature?
What are the trade-offs?
What would you improve?
```

### Cómo explicar código

Usá conectores simples:

```
First, the client sends a request.
Then, the server validates the input.
After that, the service layer processes the data.
If the request is valid, the system saves it to the database.
Finally, the API returns a response to the client.
```

### Cómo explicar una decisión técnica

```
We chose this approach because it was simpler to maintain.
The main advantage is that it reduces complexity.
The trade-off is that it may be less flexible in the future.
We considered another option, but it required more infrastructure.
```

## Sistema design básico en inglés

Si aplicás a roles backend, full stack o senior, pueden preguntarte cómo diseñar un sistema.

### Frases útiles

```
Let me start by clarifying the requirements.
I would begin with the main use cases.
The system needs to support [number] users.
The most important constraint is [latency/security/cost].
I would use a load balancer in front of the application servers.
The database would store the main user data.
A cache could help reduce repeated database calls.
We should monitor errors, latency and throughput.
```

### Mini ejemplo

> Let me start by clarifying the requirements. If we're building a URL shortener, the main use case is creating a short URL and redirecting users when they visit it. I would use a web service that generates a unique short code, stores the mapping in a database and returns the short URL. For high traffic, I would add caching for popular links and use a load balancer in front of multiple application instances.

## Preguntas para hacerle al entrevistador

Hacer preguntas demuestra interés. Prepará al menos cinco.

### Sobre el rol

```
What does a typical day look like for this role?
What are the main responsibilities for the first three months?
What would success look like in this position after six months?
What are the biggest challenges the person in this role will face?
```

### Sobre el equipo

```
How is the engineering team structured?
How does the team usually collaborate?
Do you work with Scrum, Kanban or another process?
How often does the team have code reviews or planning meetings?
```

### Sobre la tecnología

```
What technologies does the team use day to day?
Are there any plans to migrate or modernize the stack?
How do you handle testing and deployment?
What is the current approach to documentation?
```

### Sobre la cultura

```
How would you describe the company culture?
How does the company support professional growth?
How does the team handle feedback?
What do you enjoy most about working here?
```

## Cómo hablar de experiencia sin sonar inseguro

Muchas personas traducen literalmente desde el español y suenan menos profesionales.

### Evitá

```
I did a little bit of React.
I helped with some things.
I was responsible for many tasks.
I worked on a project, but I don't remember everything.
```

### Mejor

```
I worked with React to build user interfaces and connect them to APIs.
I collaborated with the team on several features.
My main responsibilities included building endpoints, reviewing code and fixing bugs.
The project involved authentication, database design and API integration.
```

## Cómo admitir que no sabés algo

No intentes inventar. En Estados Unidos, valoran la honestidad si la acompañás con capacidad de aprendizaje.

```
I don't have direct experience with that tool yet.
I haven't used it in production, but I understand the basic concept.
I would need to check the documentation before making a final decision.
I haven't worked with that exact scenario, but I would start by understanding the requirements and constraints.
```

### Ejemplo completo

> I haven't used Kubernetes in production yet, but I understand the basic concepts of containers, pods, deployments and services. I have worked with Docker, so I think I could ramp up quickly if the team uses Kubernetes.

## Errores comunes en entrevistas en inglés

### Error 1: Responder demasiado largo

Una respuesta de 3 a 5 minutos puede ser útil. Una respuesta de 10 minutos cansa al entrevistador.

#### Regla práctica

```
Pregunta simple: 30-60 segundos
Pregunta de experiencia: 1-2 minutos
Pregunta técnica compleja: 2-3 minutos
```

### Error 2: Usar palabras demasiado complejas

No necesitas sonar académico. Necesitás ser claro.

```
I implemented a solution.
I improved performance.
I reduced the number of API calls.
I collaborated with the team.
```

### Error 3: No preparar ejemplos

Si no tenés ejemplos preparados, vas a improvisar bajo presión.

Prepará estos ejemplos antes de entrevistas:

```
Un bug difícil que resolviste
Un conflicto técnico
Un proyecto del que estés orgulloso
Un error que cometiste
Una vez que mejoraste un proceso
Una vez que ayudaste a un compañero
Una vez que aprendiste algo rápido
```

### Error 4: No practicar en voz alta

Leer respuestas no es suficiente. Tenés que pronunciarlas.

Practicá así:

```
1. Elegí una pregunta.
2. Respondé en voz alta durante 90 segundos.
3. Grabate.
4. Escuchá tu respuesta.
5. Corregí claridad, velocidad y estructura.
```

## Mini banco de respuestas preparadas

### Experiencia con trabajo en equipo

> I enjoy working in teams, especially when the communication is clear. In my previous projects, I collaborated with frontend developers, designers and product people. I usually try to understand the business goal before writing code, because it helps me make better technical decisions.

### Manejo de presión

> When I'm under pressure, I try to prioritize. I identify what is critical, what can wait and what needs communication. I also try to avoid hiding problems. If there is a risk, I communicate it early and propose options.

### Aprendizaje rápido

> I'm comfortable learning new technologies. My usual process is to read the documentation, build a small example, identify the core concepts and then apply it to a real task. I also like to ask questions when I'm blocked for too long.

### Trabajo remoto

> I'm comfortable working remotely. I believe async communication is important, so I try to write clear updates, document decisions and make blockers visible. I also like having regular check-ins with the team.

Al final de la entrevista, podés decir:

```
Thank you for your time.
I enjoyed learning more about the role.
I'm very interested in the opportunity.
I'm happy to provide any additional information.
I look forward to hearing from you.
```

```
Subject: Thank you - [Role] Interview

Hi [Name],

Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today. I enjoyed learning more about the role and the engineering team.

Our conversation made me even more interested in the opportunity, especially because of the team's focus on [topic].

Please let me know if you need any additional information. I look forward to hearing from you.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

## Plan de preparación de 7 días

### Día 1: Presentación

- Escribí tu respuesta de "Tell me about yourself".
- Reducila a 90 segundos.
- Practicala 5 veces en voz alta.

### Día 2: Método STAR

- Elegí 3 historias profesionales.
- Escribilas con Situation, Task, Action y Result.
- Practicá contar cada una en 2 minutos.

### Día 3: Preguntas comunes

Practicá estas preguntas:

```
Why do you want to work here?
What are your strengths?
What are your weaknesses?
Why should we hire you?
Where do you see yourself in five years?
```

### Día 4: Técnica

- Elegí un proyecto real.
- Explicalo en inglés.
- Incluí problema, solución, tecnologías y resultado.

### Día 5: Preguntas difíciles

Practicá:

```
Tell me about a mistake.
Tell me about a conflict.
Tell me about a time you were under pressure.
Tell me about something you learned quickly.
```

### Día 6: Simulacro

- Grabate respondiendo 10 preguntas.
- Escuchá la grabación.
- Mejorá velocidad, claridad y estructura.

### Día 7: Cierre

- Prepará 5 preguntas para el entrevistador.
- Escribí tu email de seguimiento.
- Descansá y repasá solo frases clave.

## Recordatorio final

No necesitás inglés perfecto. Necesitás inglés funcional, claro y profesional.

Una buena entrevista en inglés no se trata de impresionar con vocabulario complejo. Se trata de demostrar que podés pensar, colaborar y resolver problemas.

Prepará estructuras, practicá en voz alta y recordá esto:

> Your English does not have to be perfect. Your message has to be clear.
