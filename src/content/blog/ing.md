### Vocales: Sonidos Clave

El inglés tiene **5 letras vocales** pero **más de 20 sonidos vocálicos**. Esta es la gran diferencia con el español.

| Sonido | Ejemplo          | Cómo pronunciarlo                          |
| ------ | ---------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| /æ/    | cat, bad, man    | "a" más abierta que en español             |
| /ʌ/    | cup, run, love   | Como una "a" corta y central               |
| /ɑː/   | car, far, star   | "a" larga y posterior                      |
| /ɪ/    | sit, big, fish   | "i" relajada y corta                       |
| /iː/   | see, feel, team  | "i" larga y tensa                          |
| /ʊ/    | book, good, look | "u" corta y relajada                       |
| /uː/   | food, moon, blue | "u" larga                                  |
| /ɒ/    | hot, dog, box    | "o" corta (British)                        |
| /ɔː/   | call, born, law  | "o" larga                                  |
| /eɪ/   | day, make, cake  | Diptongo "ei"                              |
| /aɪ/   | my, night, time  | Diptongo "ai"                              |
| /ɔɪ/   | boy, coin, toy   | Diptongo "oi"                              |
| /aʊ/   | now, house, out  | Diptongo "au"                              |
| /oʊ/   | go, home, know   | Diptongo "ou"                              |

## 2. LOS TIEMPOS VERBALES

### Mapa general de los tiempos

```
PASADO ◄─────────────── PRESENTE ───────────────► FUTURO

Simple Past          Simple Present         Simple Future
Past Continuous      Present Continuous     Future Continuous
Past Perfect         Present Perfect        Future Perfect
Past Perf. Cont.     Pres. Perf. Cont.      Future Perf. Cont.
```

---

### 2.1 PRESENTE SIMPLE — Simple Present

**Uso:** Hábitos, verdades generales, hechos permanentes, rutinas.

**Estructura:**

```
(+) Subject + verb (base form) / verb + s (he/she/it)
(-) Subject + don't / doesn't + verb
(?) Do / Does + subject + verb?
```

**Regla de la -s en tercera persona:**

- Mayoría de verbos: agrega **-s** → _he works, she runs_
- Verbos en -sh, -ch, -x, -o, -z: agrega **-es** → _he watches, she goes, it fixes_
- Verbos en consonante + y: cambia **-y → -ies** → _he studies, she carries_
- Excepción: _have → has_

**Ejemplos:**

```
I work every day.           Trabajo todos los días.
She doesn't like coffee.    A ella no le gusta el café.
Do you speak English?       ¿Hablás inglés?
The sun rises in the east.  El sol sale por el este.
He studies at night.        Él estudia de noche.
```

**Adverbios frecuentes:** always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never, every day/week/year

---

### 2.2 PRESENTE CONTINUO — Present Continuous

**Uso:** Acciones en progreso AHORA, situaciones temporales, planes futuros confirmados.

**Estructura:**

```
(+) Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing
(-) Subject + am/is/are + not + verb-ing
(?) Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing?
```

**Reglas del -ing:**

- Verbo termina en -e: se elimina la -e → _make → making_
- Verbo CVC (consonante-vocal-consonante) con sílaba tónica final: se duplica la última consonante → _run → running, sit → sitting_
- Verbo termina en -ie: cambia a -ying → _lie → lying_

**Ejemplos:**

```
I am working right now.         Estoy trabajando ahora mismo.
She is not coming tonight.      Ella no viene esta noche.
Are they waiting for us?        ¿Nos están esperando?
I'm meeting her tomorrow.       Me veo con ella mañana. (plan confirmado)
```

**Verbos que NO usan Present Continuous (stative verbs):**

> believe, know, understand, want, need, like, love, hate, see, hear, smell, taste, have (posesión), seem, appear, own, belong

```
✗ I am knowing the answer.
✓ I know the answer.

✗ She is wanting a coffee.
✓ She wants a coffee.
```

### 2.3 PASADO SIMPLE — Simple Past

**Uso:** Acciones completadas en un momento específico del pasado.

**Estructura:**

```
(+) Subject + verb-ed (regulares) / 2nd form (irregulares)
(-) Subject + didn't + verb (base form)
(?) Did + subject + verb (base form)?
```

**Verbos regulares → agrega -ed:**

- walk → walked, talk → talked, play → played
- Termina en -e: agrega solo -d → _like → liked_
- CVC con sílaba tónica: duplica última consonante → _stop → stopped_
- Consonante + y: cambia -y → -ied → _study → studied_

### 2.4 PASADO CONTINUO — Past Continuous

**Uso:** Acción en progreso en un momento específico del pasado. Acción interrumpida por otra.

**Estructura:** `Subject + was/were + verb-ing`

```
I was sleeping when you called.
Yo estaba durmiendo cuando llamaste.

While she was cooking, he was watching TV.
Mientras ella cocinaba, él miraba televisión.

What were you doing at 8pm yesterday?
¿Qué estabas haciendo ayer a las 8pm?
```

---

### 2.5 PRESENTE PERFECTO — Present Perfect

**Uso:** Acciones pasadas con relevancia en el presente. Experiencias de vida. Acciones recientes. Situaciones que empezaron en el pasado y continúan.

**Estructura:**

```
(+) Subject + have/has + past participle
(-) Subject + haven't/hasn't + past participle
(?) Have/Has + subject + past participle?
```

**Palabras clave:** already, yet, just, ever, never, recently, lately, since, for, so far, up to now

```
I have already eaten.              Ya comí.
She hasn't arrived yet.            Ella todavía no llegó.
Have you ever been to Japan?       ¿Alguna vez estuviste en Japón?
He has just left.                  Él acaba de irse.
I've lived here for 5 years.       Vivo acá hace 5 años. (y sigo viviendo)
They have known each other since 2010.  Se conocen desde 2010.
```

**Since vs For:**

- **Since:** desde un punto específico en el tiempo → _since Monday, since 2020, since I was a child_
- **For:** durante un período de tiempo → _for 3 hours, for 2 years, for a long time_

**Present Perfect vs Simple Past:**

| Present Perfect                                     | Simple Past                                               |
| --------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| I've seen that movie. (en algún momento de mi vida) | I saw that movie yesterday. (momento específico)          |
| She has lived in Paris. (experiencia)               | She lived in Paris from 2010 to 2015. (período definido)  |
| Have you eaten sushi? (experiencia)                 | Did you eat sushi at that restaurant? (evento específico) |

---

### 2.6 PRESENTE PERFECTO CONTINUO — Present Perfect Continuous

**Uso:** Acción que empezó en el pasado y continúa hasta ahora (énfasis en la duración).

**Estructura:** `Subject + have/has + been + verb-ing`

```
I have been studying for 3 hours.
Llevo 3 horas estudiando. (y sigo)

She has been working here since January.
Ella lleva trabajando acá desde enero.

Why are your eyes red? — I've been crying.
¿Por qué tenés los ojos rojos? — Estuve llorando.
```

---

### 2.7 PASADO PERFECTO — Past Perfect

**Uso:** Acción completada ANTES de otra acción pasada. El "pasado del pasado".

**Estructura:** `Subject + had + past participle`

```
When I arrived, she had already left.
Cuando llegué, ella ya se había ido.

He hadn't eaten before the meeting.
Él no había comido antes de la reunión.

I recognized her because I had seen her photo before.
La reconocí porque antes había visto su foto.
```

---

### 2.8 FUTURO — Simple Future

**Hay tres formas principales de expresar futuro en inglés:**

#### Will — decisiones espontáneas, predicciones, promesas, ofrecimientos

```
I'll call you tomorrow.          Te llamo mañana. (promesa)
I think it will rain.            Creo que va a llover. (predicción)
Wait, I'll help you with that.   Espera, te ayudo con eso. (decisión espontánea)
She will be 30 next year.        Ella va a cumplir 30 el año que viene.
```

#### Going to — planes ya decididos, predicciones basadas en evidencia

```
I'm going to study medicine.     Voy a estudiar medicina. (plan)
Look at those clouds — it's going to rain.  Mirá esas nubes — va a llover. (evidencia)
They're going to move to Spain.  Ellos se van a mudar a España. (plan decidido)
```

#### Present Continuous — eventos futuros con hora y lugar ya confirmados

```
I'm meeting John at 6pm.         Me veo con John a las 6pm.
We're flying to NYC tomorrow.    Mañana volamos a NYC.
```

---

### 2.9 FUTURO CONTINUO — Future Continuous

**Uso:** Acción que estará en progreso en un momento específico del futuro.

**Estructura:** `Subject + will be + verb-ing`

```
This time tomorrow, I'll be flying to London.
Mañana a esta hora, estaré volando a Londres.

Don't call at 9 — I'll be having dinner.
No llames a las 9 — voy a estar cenando.
```

---

### 2.10 FUTURO PERFECTO — Future Perfect

**Uso:** Acción que se habrá completado antes de un momento futuro.

**Estructura:** `Subject + will have + past participle`

```
By next year, I will have graduated.
Para el año que viene, me habré graduado.

She will have finished the project by Friday.
Ella habrá terminado el proyecto para el viernes.
```

## 3. VERBOS TO BE Y TO HAVE

### 3.1 TO BE — ser / estar

| Pronombre | Present | Past | Contraction         |
| --------- | ------- | ---- | ------------------- |
| I         | am      | was  | I'm                 |
| You       | are     | were | You're              |
| He/She/It | is      | was  | He's / She's / It's |
| We        | are     | were | We're               |
| You (pl.) | are     | were | You're              |
| They      | are     | were | They're             |

**Usos principales:**

```
I am a teacher.           Soy docente. (identidad/profesión)
She is tired.             Ella está cansada. (estado)
It is cold today.         Hace frío hoy. (clima)
We are from Argentina.    Somos de Argentina. (origen)
There is a problem.       Hay un problema.
There are two options.    Hay dos opciones.
```

### 3.2 TO HAVE — tener / haber

| Pronombre | Present | Past |
| --------- | ------- | ---- |
| I         | have    | had  |
| You       | have    | had  |
| He/She/It | has     | had  |
| We/They   | have    | had  |

**Usos principales:**

```
I have a car.                  Tengo un auto.
She has two brothers.          Ella tiene dos hermanos.
He had dinner at 8.            Él cenó a las 8.
We have to leave now.          Tenemos que irnos ahora. (have to = obligación)
Do you have any questions?     ¿Tienen alguna pregunta?
```

## 4. VERBOS MODALES

Los verbos modales no cambian con la persona (no llevan -s) y siempre van seguidos del verbo en forma base.

`Modal + verb (base form)`

### Tabla completa de modales

| Modal             | Uso                                           | Ejemplo                                        |
| ----------------- | --------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| **can**           | Habilidad presente, permiso informal          | I can swim / Can I leave?                      |
| **could**         | Habilidad pasada, posibilidad, permiso formal | I could run fast as a kid / Could you help me? |
| **may**           | Posibilidad, permiso formal                   | It may rain / May I come in?                   |
| **might**         | Posibilidad menor                             | I might go to the party.                       |
| **must**          | Obligación fuerte, deducción lógica           | You must wear a seatbelt / He must be tired.   |
| **have to**       | Obligación externa (reglas, circunstancias)   | I have to work tomorrow.                       |
| **should**        | Consejo, recomendación                        | You should see a doctor.                       |
| **ought to**      | Consejo (más formal que should)               | You ought to apologize.                        |
| **shall**         | Ofrecimiento, sugerencia (British)            | Shall we go? / Shall I help?                   |
| **will**          | Futuro, voluntad, promesa                     | I will call you.                               |
| **would**         | Condicional, petición educada, hábito pasado  | I would love to / Would you mind?              |
| **need to**       | Necesidad                                     | You need to rest.                              |
| **don't have to** | Falta de obligación                           | You don't have to come.                        |
| **mustn't**       | Prohibición                                   | You mustn't smoke here.                        |

### Modales para expresar certeza/posibilidad

```
100% seguro →  must be / will be       He must be at home.
90% probable → should be               She should be here by now.
50/50 →        may be / might be       It may rain later.
                                        They might come tonight.
Imposible →    can't be / couldn't be  That can't be right.
```

### Modales en pasado (modal + have + past participle)

```
must have been      → deducción del pasado     → You must have been tired.
should have done    → consejo no seguido       → You should have called me.
could have done     → posibilidad pasada       → She could have won.
might have done     → posibilidad pasada       → He might have forgotten.
can't have done     → imposibilidad pasada     → They can't have left already.
would have done     → condicional pasado       → I would have helped if I knew.
```

## 5. SUSTANTIVOS (NOUNS)

### 5.1 Plurales regulares

| Regla                                       | Singular          | Plural                |
| ------------------------------------------- | ----------------- | --------------------- |
| Agrega -s                                   | book, car, cat    | books, cars, cats     |
| Termina en -s, -x, -z, -ch, -sh: agrega -es | bus, box, watch   | buses, boxes, watches |
| Consonante + y: cambia -y → -ies            | city, baby        | cities, babies        |
| Vocal + y: agrega -s                        | day, key          | days, keys            |
| Termina en -f/-fe: cambia a -ves            | leaf, knife, wife | leaves, knives, wives |
| Termina en -o (algunos): agrega -es         | potato, tomato    | potatoes, tomatoes    |

### 5.2 Plurales irregulares

| Singular | Plural          |
| -------- | --------------- |
| man      | men             |
| woman    | women           |
| child    | children        |
| tooth    | teeth           |
| foot     | feet            |
| mouse    | mice            |
| person   | people          |
| ox       | oxen            |
| sheep    | sheep (igual)   |
| fish     | fish (igual)    |
| deer     | deer (igual)    |
| species  | species (igual) |

### 5.3 Contables vs Incontables

**Contables (countable):** Se pueden contar, tienen plural.

```
one apple, two apples, a car, some cars
```

**Incontables (uncountable):** No se pueden contar directamente, no tienen plural.

```
water, money, information, advice, news, furniture, luggage,
traffic, music, knowledge, rice, sugar, milk, air, happiness
```

> Para cuantificar incontables se usa una unidad de medida:
> _a glass of water, a piece of information, a piece of furniture, a bag of rice_

**Determinantes según contabilidad:**

| Expresión | Contables                     | Incontables                        |
| --------- | ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Algún(os) | some / any                    | some / any                         |
| Mucho/a   | many                          | much                               |
| Poco/a    | few (pocos) / a few (algunos) | little (poco) / a little (un poco) |
| Cuánto/s  | how many                      | how much                           |

## 6. ARTÍCULOS (ARTICLES)

### 6.1 A / An — artículo indefinido

- **A** antes de sonido consonántico: _a book, a university (/juː/), a European_
- **An** antes de sonido vocálico: _an apple, an hour (/aʊər/), an MBA_

> La regla es por **sonido**, no por letra.
> _an hour_ (la h es muda, suena vocal)
> _a university_ (suena /juː/, consonante)

**Usos de A/An:**

```
She is a doctor.              Es doctora. (primera mención, profesión)
I need an umbrella.           Necesito un paraguas.
He's a European citizen.      Es ciudadano europeo.
```

### 6.2 The — artículo definido

**Cuándo usar THE:**

```
The sun, the moon, the earth.         (único en su especie)
Pass me the salt.                     (ya mencionado o en contexto)
The Amazon River / The Andes.         (ríos, cadenas montañosas, mares)
The USA, the UK, the Netherlands.     (países con palabras como United/Republic)
The piano, the guitar.                (instrumentos musicales)
The rich, the poor.                   (grupos sociales con adjetivo)
She is the best student.              (superlativo)
```

**Cuándo NO usar THE (zero article):**

```
I love music.                (conceptos abstractos en general)
Breakfast is at 8.           (comidas)
She goes to school.          (instituciones usadas por su función)
I live in Argentina.         (la mayoría de países, ciudades)
I speak English.             (idiomas)
She likes cats.              (sustantivos en general)
```

## 8. ADJETIVOS (ADJECTIVES)

### 8.1 Posición de los adjetivos

En inglés, el adjetivo va ANTES del sustantivo (al revés que en español):

```
a beautiful house       una casa hermosa
an interesting movie    una película interesante
a tall, young man       un hombre alto y joven
```

### 8.2 Orden de múltiples adjetivos

Cuando hay varios adjetivos, el orden es:

**Opinión → Tamaño → Edad → Forma → Color → Origen → Material → Propósito + Sustantivo**

```
a lovely little old rectangular green French silver whittling knife
(opinión)(tamaño)(edad)(forma)(color)(origen)(material)(propósito)

a beautiful big old red Italian sports car
```

### 8.3 Comparativos y Superlativos

**Adjetivos cortos (1 sílaba):**

| Adjetivo | Comparativo | Superlativo |
| -------- | ----------- | ----------- |
| tall     | taller than | the tallest |
| big      | bigger than | the biggest |
| hot      | hotter than | the hottest |
| nice     | nicer than  | the nicest  |

**Adjetivos largos (2+ sílabas):**

| Adjetivo    | Comparativo           | Superlativo          |
| ----------- | --------------------- | -------------------- |
| beautiful   | more beautiful than   | the most beautiful   |
| expensive   | more expensive than   | the most expensive   |
| interesting | more interesting than | the most interesting |

**Irregulares:**

| Adjetivo  | Comparativo     | Superlativo           |
| --------- | --------------- | --------------------- |
| good      | better          | the best              |
| bad       | worse           | the worst             |
| far       | farther/further | the farthest/furthest |
| little    | less            | the least             |
| much/many | more            | the most              |

**Estructuras de comparación:**

```
She is taller than me.                Es más alta que yo.
He is as tall as his brother.         Es tan alto como su hermano.
This is less expensive than that.     Esto es menos caro que eso.
The more you practice, the better.    Cuanto más practicás, mejor.
```

## 9. ADVERBIOS (ADVERBS)

### 9.1 Formación de adverbios

La mayoría se forma agregando **-ly** al adjetivo:

| Adjetivo | Adverbio             |
| -------- | -------------------- |
| quick    | quickly              |
| slow     | slowly               |
| careful  | carefully            |
| easy     | easily (-y → -ily)   |
| terrible | terribly (-le → -ly) |
| happy    | happily              |

**Irregulares importantes:**

| Adjetivo | Adverbio           |
| -------- | ------------------ |
| good     | well               |
| fast     | fast (sin cambio)  |
| hard     | hard (sin cambio)  |
| early    | early (sin cambio) |
| late     | late (sin cambio)  |

### 9.2 Posición de los adverbios

```
Frecuencia: Subject + adverb + verb
  She always arrives on time.
  He never eats meat.

Modo/Lugar/Tiempo: al final de la oración
  She sings beautifully.
  I went to the store yesterday.

Antes del adjetivo o adverbio modificado:
  It's really hot today.
  She speaks very quickly.
```

### 9.3 Adverbios de frecuencia

```
always      (100%)   → I always brush my teeth.
usually     (90%)    → I usually wake up at 7.
often       (70%)    → I often go to the gym.
sometimes   (50%)    → I sometimes skip breakfast.
rarely      (20%)    → I rarely watch TV.
never       (0%)     → I never drink alcohol.
```

### 12.3 Question Tags (coletillas)

Se agregan al final de una afirmación para confirmar o pedir acuerdo:

- Oración afirmativa → tag negativo
- Oración negativa → tag afirmativo

```
It's hot today, isn't it?        Hace calor hoy, ¿no?
You live here, don't you?        Vivís acá, ¿verdad?

You won't forget, will you?      No vas a olvidar, ¿verdad?
```

### 12.4 Indirect Questions (preguntas indirectas)

Más educadas. El orden de la pregunta vuelve a ser S + V (como afirmación):

```
Direct:   Where does she live?
Indirect: Could you tell me where she lives?

Direct:   What time is it?
Indirect: Do you know what time it is?

Direct:   Is he coming?
Indirect: I wonder if he is coming.
```

## 13. CONDICIONALES

### If Clause 0 — Verdades y hechos generales

**Estructura:** `If + Present Simple, Present Simple`

```
If you heat water to 100°C, it boils.
If it rains, the streets get wet.
If you mix red and blue, you get purple.
```

### If Clause 1 — Condicional real (futuro probable)

**Estructura:** `If + Present Simple, will + verb`

```
If it rains tomorrow, I will stay home.
If you study hard, you will pass the exam.
If she calls, tell her I'm busy.
```

### If Clause 2 — Condicional irreal presente (imaginario/hipotético)

**Estructura:** `If + Past Simple, would + verb`

> Nota: Con "I/he/she/it" se usa **were** (no was) en inglés formal.

```
If I were rich, I would travel the world.
If she had more time, she would learn piano.
If he knew the answer, he would tell us.
(Implicación: él no sabe la respuesta)
```

### If Clause 3 — Condicional irreal pasado (arrepentimiento, situación hipotética pasada)

**Estructura:** `If + Past Perfect, would have + past participle`

```
If I had studied more, I would have passed.
If she had been there, she would have helped.
If they hadn't left early, they would have met him.
(Implicación: no se quedaron, entonces no lo conocieron)
```

### Mixed Conditionals — Condición pasada, resultado presente (y viceversa)

```
If I had taken that job, I would be rich now.
(pasado → presente)

If I were taller, I would have played basketball.
(presente → pasado)
```

### Otras estructuras condicionales

```
Unless  (= if not)        → Unless you hurry, we'll be late.
As long as                → You can go, as long as you're back by 10.
Provided that             → I'll help, provided that you help me too.
In case                   → Take an umbrella in case it rains.
I wish / If only          → I wish I could fly. / If only I had more money.
```

## 14. VOZ PASIVA

### Cuándo usar pasiva

- Cuando el agente (quien hace la acción) es desconocido, obvio o irrelevante
- Para dar énfasis al objeto en lugar del sujeto
- Registros formales, científicos, periodísticos

### Estructura general

`Object (nuevo sujeto) + to be (en el tiempo correcto) + past participle + (by + agent)`

### Pasiva en todos los tiempos

| Tiempo             | Activa                           | Pasiva                           |
| ------------------ | -------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Present Simple     | They make cars here.             | Cars are made here.              |
| Past Simple        | They built that bridge in 1990.  | That bridge was built in 1990.   |
| Present Continuous | They are repairing the road.     | The road is being repaired.      |
| Past Continuous    | They were filming a movie.       | A movie was being filmed.        |
| Present Perfect    | They have stolen my wallet.      | My wallet has been stolen.       |
| Past Perfect       | They had already sold the house. | The house had already been sold. |
| Future (will)      | They will announce the results.  | The results will be announced.   |
| Modal              | They must fix the problem.       | The problem must be fixed.       |

```
Active:  Someone stole my car.
Passive: My car was stolen.

Active:  The police arrested three people.
Passive: Three people were arrested by the police.

Active:  They are building a new airport.
Passive: A new airport is being built.
```

## 15. REPORTED SPEECH

Cómo reportar lo que alguien dijo. Los tiempos "retroceden" un paso.

### Cambio de tiempos (backshift)

| Directo            | Indirecto       |
| ------------------ | --------------- |
| Present Simple     | Past Simple     |
| Present Continuous | Past Continuous |
| Past Simple        | Past Perfect    |
| Present Perfect    | Past Perfect    |
| will               | would           |
| can                | could           |
| may                | might           |
| must               | had to          |

### Cambios de pronombres y expresiones de tiempo

| Directo   | Indirecto                        |
| --------- | -------------------------------- |
| I         | he/she                           |
| my        | his/her                          |
| here      | there                            |
| this      | that                             |
| today     | that day                         |
| yesterday | the day before                   |
| tomorrow  | the next day / the following day |
| now       | then                             |
| last week | the week before                  |
| next week | the following week               |

### Ejemplos

```
Direct:   "I love you," he said.
Indirect:  He said (that) he loved her.

Direct:   "I'm working," she said.
Indirect:  She said (that) she was working.

Direct:   "I have finished," he told me.
Indirect:  He told me (that) he had finished.

Direct:   "I will come tomorrow," she said.
Indirect:  She said (that) she would come the next day.
```

### Reported Questions

```
Direct:   "Where do you live?" she asked.
Indirect:  She asked where I lived.

Direct:   "Are you coming?" he asked.
Indirect:  He asked if/whether I was coming.
```

### Reported Commands and Requests

```
Direct:   "Close the door," she said.
Indirect:  She told me to close the door.

Direct:   "Please don't go," he said.
Indirect:  He asked me not to go.
```

## 19. PRONUNCIACIÓN Y FONÉTICA

### 19.1 Consonantes difíciles para hispanohablantes

#### TH — dos sonidos distintos

**TH sonoro /ð/** (como una "d" vibrante):

> the, this, that, these, those, they, them, there, then, though, mother, brother, father, weather, together

**TH sordo /θ/** (como una "z" castellana):

> think, thank, thing, three, throw, bath, math, mouth, tooth, truth, path

**Truco:** Para ambos, sacá la lengua entre los dientes y soplá aire. Si vibra la garganta = sonoro (/ð/). Si no vibra = sordo (/θ/).

#### R — nunca es vibrante como en español

La **R inglesa** es aproximante. La lengua se dobla hacia atrás sin tocar el paladar.

> red, run, rain, road, brown, great, tree, dream

#### W — no existe en español

Labios redondeados, como para decir "u" pero sin vocalizar y luego abriendo:

> water, window, work, world, week, why, what, where, when

#### V — no es "b"

El **V** inglés requiere el labio inferior tocando los dientes superiores:

> very, voice, love, live, river, seven, never

#### Diferencia V / B:

> **very** ≠ **berry** / **vine** ≠ **bine** / **vet** ≠ **bet**

### 19.2 Sílabas tónicas (Word Stress)

El inglés es una lengua con ritmo de ESTRÉS. Las sílabas acentuadas son más fuertes, más largas y más claras. Las no acentuadas se reducen.

```
PHOtograph / phoTOgraphy / photoGRAphic
(misma raíz, diferente acento según la forma)

REcord (sustantivo) / reCORD (verbo)
PERmit (sustantivo) / perMIT (verbo)
PREsent (sustantivo/adjetivo) / preSENT (verbo)
OBject (sustantivo) / obJECT (verbo)
```

**Reglas generales de acentuación:**

- Sustantivos de 2 sílabas: generalmente en la 1ra → TAble, PEOple, MOney
- Verbos de 2 sílabas: generalmente en la 2da → beLIEVE, forGET, reLAX
- Sufijo -ion: acento en la sílaba anterior → inforMAtion, eduCAtion, orGAnization

### 19.3 Linking (Enlace de palabras)

En inglés fluido, las palabras se enlazan. No se pronuncian como bloques separados.

```
"an apple" suena como "anapple"
"turn it off" suena como "turnitoff"
"I need it" suena como "Ineedit"
"What are you doing?" suena como "Whaddaya doin'?"
```

### 19.4 Contracciones en habla rápida

```
going to  → gonna        I'm gonna call you.
want to   → wanna        I wanna go home.
have to   → hafta        I hafta work tomorrow.
out of    → outta        Get outta here!
kind of   → kinda        It's kinda weird.
sort of   → sorta        I'm sorta busy.
```

### 19.5 Silent Letters (Letras mudas)

| Letra muda                         | Ejemplos                                             |
| ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| **K** antes de N                   | know, knife, knock, knee, knight                     |
| **W** antes de R                   | write, wrong, wrap, wrist, wrestle                   |

### 20.2 Errores gramaticales frecuentes

**1. Pronombre sujeto obligatorio**

```
✗ Is raining.          → ✓ It is raining.
✗ Is very hot.         → ✓ It is very hot.
✗ Is necessary to...   → ✓ It is necessary to...
```

**2. Do/Does en preguntas — no invertir el orden sin auxiliar**

```
✗ Where you live?      → ✓ Where do you live?
✗ What she wants?      → ✓ What does she want?
✗ Why you did that?    → ✓ Why did you do that?
```

**3. Doble negación**

```
✗ I don't know nothing. → ✓ I don't know anything.
✗ I haven't seen nobody. → ✓ I haven't seen anybody.
```

**4. Make vs Do**

- **Do:** actividades, tareas, trabajo general → _do homework, do the dishes, do business_
- **Make:** crear, producir, causar → _make a cake, make a decision, make a mistake, make money_

```
✗ I did a mistake.     → ✓ I made a mistake.
✗ Make your homework.  → ✓ Do your homework.
✗ She did a cake.      → ✓ She made a cake.
```

**5. Say vs Tell**

- **Say** + something (sin objeto personal o con "to someone")
- **Tell** + someone + something (siempre con objeto personal)

```
✓ He said "hello."
✓ He said (that) he was tired.
✓ He told me he was tired.
✗ He told that he was tired. (falta el objeto)
✗ He said me he was tired. (say no lleva objeto directo de persona)
```

**6. Ago vs Before**

- **Ago:** desde ahora hacia el pasado → _two years ago, 5 minutes ago_
- **Before:** antes de un momento del pasado → _two years before that_

```
✗ I met her two years before.  → ✓ I met her two years ago.
```

**7. During vs For**

- **During:** + sustantivo de período → _during the movie, during summer_
- **For:** + período de tiempo → _for 2 hours, for 3 days_

```
✗ I slept during 8 hours.  → ✓ I slept for 8 hours.
✓ I fell asleep during the movie.
```

**8. Been to vs Gone to**

- **Have been to:** fui y ya volví → _I've been to Paris._ (ya estoy de vuelta)
- **Have gone to:** fue y todavía no volvió → _She's gone to Paris._ (sigue allá)

**9. Forget vs Leave**

- **Forget:** no recordar algo → _I forgot my keys._ (no los recuerdo)
- **Leave:** dejar algo en un lugar → _I left my keys at home._ (sé dónde están)

```
¿CUÁNDO?          ¿QUÉ TIEMPO?                    ¿EJEMPLO?

PRESENTE

Experiencia/
resultado hoy   → Present Perfect             → She has worked here for 3 years.

PASADO
Momento espec.  → Simple Past                 → She worked yesterday.

Espontáneo/

```

